Preamble

The Democratic Party of the United States of America aims to uphold the long-lasting values of equality in all peoples of the United States, and maintain the indispensable quality of liberty and freedom in all her citizens. We the Democratic Party, in order to establish a more perfect democracy, to secure justice, insure domestic unity, and provide for common defense, do ordain and establish this Manifesto for the United States of America.

In the 240 years since this nation has been recognized, we have journeyed on a great path to prosperity. Under the leadership of our esteemed Democratic presidents, we have come through thick and thin, braced ourselves against internal and external storms, and persevered still. With the leadership of President Barack Obama, and through the hard work of the American people, we hurdled over the Great Recession. The private-sector growth in American businesses have now added over 15 million jobs since 2010. Twenty million people have gained health insurance coverage as a result of Obama-care. And the American auto industry, rich with history, is booming more than ever.

However, despite how far we’ve come, too many Americans have been left behind. Issues of wage gap, racial divide, inequality, and the rigidity of the social structure have all continued to worsen as the cost of everything from small commodities to college education continues to rise. As honest citizens struggle, the top 1% accrues more wealth and more power. Our well-meaning Republican friends in Congress have chosen to go against the Constitutional values of equality, and idly watch as dysfunction and corruption spread over the solutions for the challenges we face.

We, the Democratic Party believe that cooperation is above conflict, unity is stronger than division, empowerment is better than resentment, and that bridges are better than walls.

It is a powerful idea that we uphold: in unity, we are strong.

 

 

Providing Quality and Affordable Education

America is the land of equal opportunity. In the present age, education is the basis of such equality of opportunity. Higher education should without doubt be encouraged, not defamed as a thieving scam, to be considered as analogously infamous as the debt it inevitably brings. Every American should be able to reach for their dreams and pertain their happiness through higher education, but the question of student loans plays an integral part in that decision. The Democratic Party’s stance on student loans is that they should still be offered by the federal government. The problem that causes the student debt crisis is not the existence of a federal loan itself, rather, it is the public’s understanding of student loans that must be revised and improved. As long the public is made well aware of risks and precautions of student loans as well as loaning itself with full transparency, we can be sure that the benefits of higher education and student loans come with minimal risks. To ensure that every student loan is taken seriously, as well as in maintaining the opportunity for every individual to be able to reach for their dreams, funding for public education should only be raised, not cut down.

 

The Democratic Party will look to repeal the plan made during the Trump administration to cut $10.6 billion from federal education initiatives. With this funding withdrawn from public schools, a vast majority of the funding was used to fund private and religious schools ($400 million), with only 1 billion given to public schools to expand, and the remainder of the 10.6 billion sum used as retribution for government activities after several tax cuts on the rich.

This plan may be detrimental to future expansion and progress to be made in public schools, the essential institutions where the mass majority of the American populous relies on for education (50.7 million students out of a total of 56.6 million in 2018).

 

The next step would be to sustain federal loans. Since private loans cost much more and have much less favorable terms compared to federal loans, (and are generally only used when students have exhausted the borrowing limit under federal student loans), it can only be reasonable that the federal government can step in and alleviate the debt situation. Private loans are not eligible for Income Based Repayment plans, and frequently have less flexible payment terms, higher fees, and more penalties – all in essence a part of the “hidden fees” that private banks have devised as profiteering strategies over the years, targeting those most vulnerable in the working class. Thus, federal loans are the only way to guarantee that students are given fair and equal starting grounds in higher education.

 

Next on our agenda is to increase the funding amassed in the US Department of Education, specifically to office of Federal Student Aid. The plan itself will not increase the amount of funds available for student loans ready for distribution, but rather to increase public understanding of the benefits and risks of taking student loans: allowing individuals to understand which types of loans are most suitable, and how to make a sustainable plan for the future after education. We will also look to allow those who currently have student debt to refinance their loans at the lowest rates possible. We will simplify and expand access to income-based repayment so that no student loan borrowers ever have to pay more than they can afford. 

This could be enacted through the form of programs, such as the recent Utah state program, that requires all colleges and universities in Utah to notify students who have a loan to look up their federal loan payoff amount and calculate their estimated monthly payments at least annually.

 Other organizations, such as the Student Loan Assistance foundation, the Public Service Loan Forgiveness, loan discharge programs begun by the Obama Administration and other non-governmental organizations which provide assistance in the relevant area, will receive government backing.

 

The Democratic Party is committed to eliminating inequality, and particularly those that bar students from low-income communities from receiving the same American education that we all uphold. We will advocate for labor and public assistance laws that will make sure that families with unstable economic backgrounds can spend time together while raising household incomes in poor communities. We will ensure that our children have health care, stable housing free of contaminants, and communities free of violence. These will be among other concrete steps in reducing the curriculum gap in the schools across America in more standardized education through incentivized government programs.

 

To protect equality of opportunity, the Democratic Party will gather efforts in cracking down on unfair advantages in the college admissions process into public and state institutions. We, as a country founded upon innovators and pioneers, must continue to ensure that money cannot be an automatic backdoor into higher education, and that the quality of such selection is standardized and fair for all. Cheating the system cannot be brushed aside, and while we respect the privacy of private institutions, we must not let them abuse this privacy for the benefit of the elite.

 

We will also continue to crack down on for-profit schools that take millions in federal financial aid—often as their principal source of revenue—and then exploit students and burden them with debt rather than educating them. That is why we will strengthen the gainful employment rule to ensure that for-profit schools enable students to complete their degrees and prepare them for work. We will go after for-profits that engage in deceptive marketing, fraud, and other illegal practices. We will also exercise our responsibility in oversight over the Department of Education to carry out their obligation to close down those for-profit schools that consistently engage in fraudulent and illegal conduct. It is unacceptable that for-profit schools with low graduation rates keep encouraging their students to take out federal loans they will have trouble paying back. Therefore, to the maximum extent possible, we will also encourage widespread relief and the forgiveness of debt to those students harmed by the practices of fraudulent institutions.

 

Lastly, we look to restore the prior standard in bankruptcy law to allow borrowers with student loans to be able to discharge their debts in bankruptcy as a measure of last resort. This will not only allow the process of bankruptcy to represent its full intents and purpose, but also to provide primary incentive for banks to provide student loans only on the terms that their education is capable of allowing them to repay the sum, once again initiating the regulation process between universities and banks to keep the other institution in check for the wellbeing of oneself.

 

 

 

 

 

Preventing Gun Violence

33,000 Americans die every year due to gun related crimes. Yet the ownership of firearms is one of the most important and renowned aspects of the American practice. Gun violence is an evil that no man, woman, or child should ever have to face, yet it is an evil that has already touched the lives of millions of Americans, directly or indirectly. We, as the Democratic Party, feels the need to clarify that our stance on gun control is not to confiscate and regulate all firearms available to citizens, but to simply increase regulations upon the right to own a firearm to avoid gun violence. Keeping communities safe and respecting the rights of gun owners are not mutually exclusive. In order to limit the possibilities of gun violence we believe that the right to own a firearm is subject to reasonable regulation. And to that we believe an honest, open national conversation of firearms and its future in America. The Democratic Party believes that we must take immediate action to address gun violence, to save American lives.

 

The Democratic Party looks to repeal the Protection of Lawful Commerce in Arms Act, aimed to target high-risk immunity protections on gun makers and gun sellers to stop the bleeding at its source, especially cracking down on assault weapons (weapons of war) manufacturers and traders in consideration of the risk it poses, rather than protection offered.

The Democratic Party in its most controversial step will repeal state control of gun law minimums to establish national standard in gun safety. As the laws regarding gun violence in the United States is only as strong as that of its most loosely regulated state. However, this federal intervention will only establish bare minimums in gun ownership, should individual states feel the need to heighten their security, that is still up to the individual state.

The democratic party will also invest in further research into effective gun-violence prevention policies.

 

Revised Gun Control Proposal

Purchasing a firearm

  • National Standard established

Minimum requirements for handgun purchase from licensed retailer

  • 18 years of age
  • Proper Identification
  • Background Check
  • Medical Check – Checking for mental illnesses, eyesight, etc.
  • Judgement test on gun laws to obtain learner’s license
  • 6 hours of required training on safety to obtain Firearm license
  • Paperwork for record of purchase

Concealed Carry License

  • Establish minimal national standard

Minimum requirements:

  • 18 years of age
  • Proper Identification
  • Firearm License
  • 6 additional hours of required training
  • Fingerprint Required
  • Paperwork for record of purchase

Private Sales

Minimum requirements:

  • Firearm License
  • Paperwork for record of purchase

Gun Show Loopholes

  • All purchases require paperwork for record of purchase

 

Immigration System

America was founded by immigrants of different ethnicities, cultures, and languages; its development was fueled by the influx of immigrant laborers, from the Westward Expansion to the Industrial Revolution. Our national identity can only be strengthened with immigration. However, more than 11 million immigrants without legal documents are within our borders, a precarious situation which tears families apart and prevent undocumented workers from ever being anything else.

 

The participants in the DACA program repealed by Trump in 2017 facing the coming threat of deportation in 2020 is our priority to address. As numerous studies have shown, among many programs, DACA is one that is beneficial for not only the participants but also the overall American economy and society. Statistically, these immigrants already living in the United States, who are assets to their communities and contribute so much to our country, should be incorporated completely into our society through legal processes. They help to bridge the American recognition of immigrants with the reality that these immigrants have already made home in the US and therefore would be impractical and unnecessary to be deported from the US. To prioritize the mutual good for both the immigrants and the American, we demand the reactivation of DACA and the enforcement of its effects by collaborating with United We Dream and the ACLU, making sure that the participants could make the best out of their new rights (social security number etc.) in education and employment.

 

While we advocate for open-border policies in terms of DACA and TPS reactivation and visa promotion, we must address the potential threat of terrorism by turning our targets from ethnic groups to individuals. For the former is only contributive in rendering the US with the image of a conservative, racist image.

 

Firstly, we should encourage information sharing with other countries to mark individuals who are suspected in not just our borders. Terrorism is no longer an issue faced by isolated nations, but by the international community. To combat international terrorism, one must harness international efforts. The Democratic Party will look to implement TTIC system (Terrorist Tracking and International Communication) that will allow all nations to take part in further security in their borders.

This system will encourage the sharing of information to mark individuals who are suspected in more than one country. Other government initialized programs such as the NAILS (National Automated Immigration Lookout System) database will also be on track to expand by becoming incorporated as a sister institution of the Federal Bureau of Investigation.

 

Next, we should expand the NAILS (National Automated Immigration Lookout System) by incorporating information from FBI to build a more comprehensive database for tracking down terrorists.

 

As the current administration is posing a variety of challenges on employment and investment-based visa applicants, we demand to lift those challenges and lower the bar. We must recognize the significance working immigrants have on our economy and society. Not only is our innovation economy driven by the breakthroughs and labor supply made possible by the employment-based immigrants, a great portion of the funding for our private businesses and government projects are contributed by the investment-based immigrants. Instead of making the process unnecessarily complex, we should promote easier application processes for H-1B, EB, and L visas.

 

Lastly, the Democratic Party will support the reactivation of TPS (temporary protection status).
